Private Student Halls

If you do not (or cannot) get an offer for a room in university halls, you can secure a room from private student halls.

Advantages

  • More options and often easier to get into than university halls
  • No "preference" system: get what you apply for
  • More trustworthy than private housing from individual landlords
  • Rent a place with friends and share utility costs
  • No viewing necessary, so a popular choice for internationals

Disadvantages

  • Not affiliated with the university, so no room guarantee
  • Not necessarily up to the quality guaranteed by university halls
  • Often more expensive than university halls

Finding a private hall

There are many private hall letting companies. It's very important to do your research on their authenticity. Here are some well-known ones:

Private Housing

Edinburgh also offers a large number of flats, either rented by agencies or private landlords. These are often within the old and traditional tenement buildings and offer yet another interesting living experience. Options are vast, however flats go quickly and agencies and landlords often receive multiple requests. In general, agencies will want you to view the property first. After you viewed the property and inform your agent of your choice, they normally get back to you that you can reserve the property by paying a holding deposit. Once you pay this holding deposit, the agency will then prepare a contract for you that you should read carefully before signing it. It is also worth noting that you might require a guarantor to proof that you can pay your rent. Otherwise, you can often pay 6 months in advance.

Cost

The cost for private rented accommodation varies, but is generally between £300-400 per month for a room or £500-600 per month for a one-bedroom flat, not including bills and utilities. Set a clear budget of how much you are willing to pay when looking for flats to avoid signing a lease on a flat you cannot afford.

If you prefer to pay rent and utilities together, there are also several private student-only accommodations run by private companies, typically going for £130-150 per week, or £520-600 per month.

Finding a place

To find suitable flats or rooms, it is a good idea to check the following websites regularly. Some of these allow you to save searches and set alerts for your search criteriat that will sent an automated email to yourself.

Private rented accommodations:

  • Studentpad
  • Accommodation for Students
  • Edinburgh Room Rentals
  • Letting Web
  • City Lets
  • Gumtree
  • Rightmove
  • Zoopla
  • Shareroom

You may also need to arrive a few weeks prior to your desired move-in date in order to allot enough time for viewings and finalising lease agreements, as many rented accommodations will require an in-person viewing first. Other places to look out for accommodation listings include newspapers, noticeboards around the city and a dedicated facebook group called "EdinRoom". Additionally, many landlords will post “For Rent” signs outside of available flats, so it may be worthwhile to walk through some of your desired neighbourhoods.

Stay safe!

Important: avoid scams

  • Be sure to view the accommodation prior to sending money to ensure that the listing is genuine. A common scam is to ask for a deposit upfront before viewing in order to secure the accommodation. Verify that the landlord is genuine as well before parting with any money.
  • You can find out if the landlord and the property are registered by visiting the Landord Registration Scotland website and entering the address of the property. If you cannot find the property then you can call or email the Council's landlord registration team to double check on 0131 469 5293 or email them at landlordregistration@edinburgh.gov.uk. If they are not registered, you should not rent from them.
  • If you would like to check if your letting agency is legitimate you could check the following websites, although membership here is voluntary.
    • Association of Rental Letting Agents
    • National Approved Letting Scheme
    • The Property Ombudsman
  • If you have been the victim of a scam you can contact the University police team here.

Know your rights as a tenant:

  • Terms, conditions and guides to renting: www.rentingscotland.org
  • You may be required to have a UK guarantor, as proof of funding. If you are an international student or do not have access to a guarantor, you may be asked to pay 3-6 months worth of rent upfront in order to prove your financial status.
  • Students are generally exempt from paying Council Tax, provided that you study for more than 21 hours per week for more than 24 weeks per year. See the Council Tax website for more information.
  • Read your lease carefully before signing. Photograph the state of the flat when you first move in and bring any issues found up to the landlord in writing, in case of later dispute. Request a receipt for your deposit.

FAQ Guide

University student halls

  • How do I apply for accommodation?
    • Must be made separately from any application to study at the University of Edinburgh.
  • When should I apply for accommodation?
    • Anytime from February 2017; whether your place of study is unconditional, conditional or insurance.
    • Apply by 16 August 2017 in order to meet the conditions of the accommodation guarantee.
    • Students studying in Semester 2 only, commencing in January 2018, should only apply for accommodation after 1 October 2017.
  • Will I be offered my first preference?
    • Only students who require a particular type of accommodation due to special needs are guaranteed to be offered first preference.
  • When will I get the result of my application?
    • We will acknowledge all applications on receipt.
    • Offers of accommodation made from June 2017
  • How will I pay for my accommodation?
    • Rents are typically payable via a range of flexible options which are set up at the point you accept your offer
    • Options: payment in full or by semester/monthly instalment plans using either direct debit or recurring credit card payments.
  • If I reject my offer of accommodation, will I get another?
    • Unless there are special circumstances, we will be unable to make you another offer.
  • What if I don’t qualify for an offer?
    • Unfortunately, we are not able to guarantee accommodation to those students who do not meet the criteria.
    • However, you can still apply online and be placed on the waiting list.
  • I live in Edinburgh, can I still apply to move into Halls?
    • University accommodation is not guaranteed if you already live in Edinburgh or close to it.
    • You can still apply though and might be offered University accommodation if there are spaces.

      Private student halls

      • How do I book my student accommodation?
        • You can visit the website of one of the private halls presented in the private halls section. Search through their listings and choose your desired property. Fill in all the required information and confirm the booking.
      • Can I cancel my booking?
        • Yes, most providers allow cancellation. Please read the cancellation policies of the property and the provider for precise information.
      • Can I get refund?
        • Yes, most providers allow cancellation. Please read the cancellation policies of the property and the provider for precise information.
      • Do I need a guarantor?
        • This depends on the provider but in most cases the properties require a guarantor. The guarantor is the responsible person if you have any financial issues with the property.
      • Can I visit the room before booking it?
        • Yes. Most of the properties can be visited beforehand.
      • Can I pay the rent in instalments?
        • This depends on the property but in most cases paying in installments is an option. Please check the different policies for further information.
      • Does the rent include service bills?
        • Yes, most properties include all service bills. Please check the different policies for further information.

      Private housing

      • How do I search for a room?
        • Visit the homepages of the sites in the private housing section. Fill in the information and check the different options available. Alternatively, contact a housing agency in Edinburgh but be aware of the fees that apply for agents. Whichever option you choose, check for new ads daily and contact the landlord as fast as possible. The renting market in Edinburgh is very competitive and you have to be fast.
      • Should I visit the property before renting it?
        • Yes. To find a room or a flat you should come to Edinburgh and arrange visits. Serious landlords and agencies will not rent out a property without having met you in person.
      • Do I have to sign a contract?
        • Absolutely, yes. The contract ensures that you are the legal tenant of the property. It protects both parties, you and the landlord. Meet the landlord in person to sign the contract.
      • Do I have to pay a deposit?
        • Yes, in most cases the landlord wants the tenant to pay a deposit to ensure that any damages can be taken care of. Usually, the deposit is a one or two months’ rent. If no damages were done, you get the full amount back at the end of your tenancy.
      • Should I pay the deposit before signing in contract?
        • No. Never pay anything in advance before seeing the place and signing a contract. This is definitely a scam; serious landlords never require a deposit before signing a contract and seeing you in person.
      • Do I need a guarantor?
        • In most cases and with all agencies you require a guarantor based in the UK. The guarantor is the responsible person if you have any financial issues with the property.
