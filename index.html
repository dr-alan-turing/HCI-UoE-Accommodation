  • Taught postgrads within the EU (including UK)

Stay safe!

Important: avoid scams

  • Be sure to view the accommodation prior to sending money to ensure that the listing is genuine. A common scam is to ask for a deposit upfront before viewing in order to secure the accommodation. Verify that the landlord is genuine as well before parting with any money.
  • You can find out if the landlord and the property are registered by visiting the Landord Registration Scotland website and entering the address of the property. If you cannot find the property then you can call or email the Council's landlord registration team to double check on 0131 469 5293 or email them at landlordregistration@edinburgh.gov.uk. If they are not registered, you should not rent from them.
  • If you would like to check if your letting agency is legitimate you could check the following websites, although membership here is voluntary.
    • Association of Rental Letting Agents
    • National Approved Letting Scheme
    • The Property Ombudsman
  • If you have been the victim of a scam you can contact the University police team here.

Know your rights as a tenant:

  • Terms, conditions and guides to renting: www.rentingscotland.org
  • You may be required to have a UK guarantor, as proof of funding. If you are an international student or do not have access to a guarantor, you may be asked to pay 3-6 months worth of rent upfront in order to prove your financial status.
  • Students are generally exempt from paying Council Tax, provided that you study for more than 21 hours per week for more than 24 weeks per year. See the Council Tax website for more information.
  • Read your lease carefully before signing. Photograph the state of the flat when you first move in and bring any issues found up to the landlord in writing, in case of later dispute. Request a receipt for your deposit.

Cost

The cost for private rented accommodation varies, but is generally between £300-400 per month for a room or £500-600 per month for a one-bedroom flat, not including bills and utilities. Set a clear budget of how much you are willing to pay when looking for flats to avoid signing a lease on a flat you cannot afford.

If you prefer to pay rent and utilities together, there are also several private student-only accommodations run by private companies, typically going for £130-150 per week, or £520-600 per month.

FAQ Guide

University student halls

  • How do I apply for accommodation?
    • Must be made separately from any application to study at the University of Edinburgh.
  • When should I apply for accommodation?
    • Anytime from February 2017; whether your place of study is unconditional, conditional or insurance.
    • Apply by 16 August 2017 in order to meet the conditions of the accommodation guarantee.
    • Students studying in Semester 2 only, commencing in January 2018, should only apply for accommodation after 1 October 2017.
  • Will I be offered my first preference?
    • Only students who require a particular type of accommodation due to special needs are guaranteed to be offered first preference.
  • When will I get the result of my application?
    • We will acknowledge all applications on receipt.
    • Offers of accommodation made from June 2017
  • How will I pay for my accommodation?
    • Rents are typically payable via a range of flexible options which are set up at the point you accept your offer
    • Options: payment in full or by semester/monthly instalment plans using either direct debit or recurring credit card payments.
  • If I reject my offer of accommodation, will I get another?
    • Unless there are special circumstances, we will be unable to make you another offer.
  • What if I don’t qualify for an offer?
    • Unfortunately, we are not able to guarantee accommodation to those students who do not meet the criteria.
    • However, you can still apply online and be placed on the waiting list.
